1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide to Us

We may collect information that you voluntarily provide to us when you:

  • Register for an account on our mobile applications or website
  • Fill out forms or contact us for inquiries
  • Subscribe to our newsletter
  • Participate in surveys, contests, or promotions
  • Make purchases or transactions
  • Upload or submit content through our services

This information may include:

  • Name, email address, phone number, and postal address
  • Company name and job title (if applicable)
  • Payment information (processed securely through third-party payment processors)
  • Login credentials and account information
  • Business information relevant to our agricultural products and services

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you use our services, we may automatically collect certain information, including:

  • Device information (device type, operating system, unique device identifiers)
  • Usage data (features accessed, time spent, click patterns)
  • Location data (with your permission)
  • Log data (IP address, browser type, pages visited)
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies

1.3 Information from Third Parties

We may receive information from third parties, including:

  • Business partners and service providers
  • Advertising networks and analytics providers
  • Social media platforms (when you connect or interact with us through them)
  • Public databases and data providers

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Service Delivery: To provide, maintain, and improve our services
  • Account Management: To create and manage your account
  • Transactions: To process purchases, orders, and payments
  • Communication: To send you updates, newsletters, and marketing communications (with your consent)
  • Customer Support: To respond to your inquiries and provide technical support
  • Personalization: To personalize your experience and provide tailored content
  • Analytics: To analyze usage patterns and improve our services
  • Advertising: To display relevant advertisements through ad networks
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with legal obligations and protect our rights
  • Fraud Prevention: To detect and prevent fraud and unauthorized activities

4. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information, including:

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest
  • Regular security assessments and audits
  • Access controls and authentication measures
  • Secure data centers with physical security
  • Employee training on data protection
  • Incident response and breach notification procedures

While we strive to protect your information, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information:

5.1 General Rights

  • Access: Request a copy of your personal information
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your information (subject to legal requirements)
  • Restriction: Request restriction of processing in certain circumstances
  • Portability: Request transfer of your data in a portable format
  • Objection: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent

5.2 Communication Preferences

You can manage your communication preferences by:

  • Contacting us directly to update your preferences

5.3 Cookie Preferences

You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings or our cookie consent management platform.

By using our services, you consent to the transfer of your information to countries including, but not limited to, the United States, China, and other jurisdictions where our servers and service providers are located.

10.2 Types of Ads We Display

We display the following types of advertisements in our applications:

10.2.1 Splash Ads (App Open Ads)

When you open our applications, you may see a splash ad or app open ad displayed briefly. These ads:

  • Appear during app launch or when returning to the app from background
  • Are displayed for a limited duration (typically 3-5 seconds)
  • May include a close button after the initial display period
  • Help support free app access for all users

Splash ads may be served by any of our advertising partners including Google AdMob, Unity Ads, Facebook Audience Network, AppLovin MAX, IronSource, Pangle, AdColony, Chartboost, and other networks.

10.2.2 Rewarded Video Ads

Our applications may offer rewarded video ads that provide benefits such as:

  • Extra in-app currency or credits
  • Access to premium features temporarily
  • Extended trial periods for paid features
  • Bonus points or rewards in gamified experiences

Rewarded videos are optional and require user initiation. You can opt out of rewarded ads through your app settings or by declining individual offers.

10.2.3 Interstitial Ads

Full-screen advertisements that appear:

  • Between levels or game stages
  • During natural transitions in the app
  • Before accessing certain features
  • At appropriate intervals during content consumption

Interstitial ads include a visible close button and should not interfere with core functionality.

10.2.4 Banner Ads

Standard banner advertisements displayed:

  • At the top or bottom of screens
  • Consistently throughout app usage
  • In non-intrusive positions that don't obstruct content

Banner ads refresh periodically and may display targeted or contextual advertising.

10.2.5 Native Ads

Advertisements designed to match the look and feel of our applications:

  • Appear within content feeds
  • Clearly labeled as "Sponsored" or "Advertisement"
  • May include images, text, and call-to-action buttons
  • Designed to be less intrusive than traditional ad formats

10.3 How We Use Advertising Data

Advertising networks and platforms may collect and use data for the following purposes:

  • Ad Delivery: To serve relevant advertisements based on user interests and behavior
  • Targeting: To personalize ads based on demographics, interests, and usage patterns
  • Measurement: To measure ad effectiveness and campaign performance
  • Optimization: To improve ad selection algorithms and user experience
  • Frequency Capping: To limit how often users see the same ad
  • Attribution: To track conversions and measure advertising ROI

10.4 Data Collection by Ad Networks

Our advertising partners may collect:

  • Device identifiers (GAID, IDFA)
  • Device type and specifications
  • Operating system and version
  • App usage and interaction data
  • Location data (with consent)
  • IP address and network information
  • Advertising identifiers and reset preferences
  • Interest-based advertising data

10.5 Advertising Identifier Management

On mobile devices, advertising identifiers (GAID on Android, IDFA on iOS) are used to deliver targeted advertising. You can:

  • Reset or Limit Ad Tracking: Use your device settings to reset or limit ad tracking
  • Opt-Out Mechanisms: Utilize opt-out options provided by advertising networks
  • Privacy Dashboards: Check Google's Privacy Sandbox or Apple's App Tracking Transparency settings

10.6 Consent Management for Advertising

For users in jurisdictions requiring consent for advertising (such as the EEA/UK under GDPR), we implement consent management platforms that:

  • Present clear information about advertising practices
  • Allow users to opt in or out of various advertising categories
  • Support granular consent choices for different advertising purposes
  • Remember and respect user preferences across sessions
  • Comply with IAB Europe Transparency and Consent Framework

10.7 Interest-Based Advertising

We and our advertising partners may engage in interest-based advertising, which involves:

  • Creating profiles based on user interests and behaviors
  • Collecting data across websites and apps
  • Serving ads based on inferred interests
  • Sharing data with multiple advertising platforms

You can opt out of interest-based advertising through:

  • Device settings (Limit Ad Tracking on iOS, Opt out of Ads Personalization on Android)
  • Industry opt-out tools (such as the DAA or EDAA opt-out pages)
  • Individual advertising network settings

10.8 Ad Verification and Quality

We work with third-party ad verification services to ensure:

  • Brand safety for advertisers
  • Prevention of fraudulent clicks and impressions
  • Viewability measurement
  • Compliance with advertising standards and guidelines

10.9 Your Advertising Choices

You have several options to control advertising:

  • Device Settings: Enable "Limit Ad Tracking" (iOS) or "Opt out of Ads Personalization" (Android)
  • In-App Settings: Many of our apps offer advertising preferences in settings
  • Web Browser: Use browser settings to manage cookies and tracking
  • Industry Opt-Outs: Visit opt-out pages for major advertising networks
  • Subscription: Consider our premium ad-free subscription options where available

12. Regional Privacy Rights

We provide additional privacy protections and rights to users in certain jurisdictions.

12.1 European Economic Area (EEA) and United Kingdom

For users in the EEA and UK, we comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and UK GDPR:

  • Lawful Basis: We process data based on legitimate interests, contract performance, consent, or legal obligations
  • Data Subject Rights: Full access to rights including access, rectification, erasure, portability, and objection
  • Data Protection Officer: Contact details for our Data Protection Officer are provided below
  • Supervisory Authority: Right to lodge complaints with local data protection authorities
  • Privacy Shield/Transfer Mechanisms: Use of Standard Contractual Clauses for international transfers
  • Record of Processing: Maintain records of processing activities as required by GDPR

12.2 California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

For California residents, we comply with the CCPA:

  • Right to Know: Right to know what personal information is collected and how it's used
  • Right to Delete: Right to request deletion of personal information
  • Right to Opt-Out: Right to opt out of the sale of personal information
  • Non-Discrimination: Right not to be discriminated against for exercising privacy rights
  • Verifiable Requests: Process verified consumer requests within 45 days

Note: We do not sell personal information in the traditional sense. However, sharing data with advertising partners may be considered a "sale" under CCPA. Users can opt out of this sharing.

12.3 Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act (VCDPA)

For Virginia residents, we comply with the VCDPA:

  • Right to confirm whether data is processed and access such data
  • Right to correct inaccuracies in personal data
  • Right to delete personal data
  • Right to obtain a copy of personal data in a portable format
  • Right to opt out of targeted advertising, data sales, and profiling

12.4 Colorado Privacy Act (CPA)

For Colorado residents, we comply with the CPA:

  • Right to opt out of processing for targeted advertising
  • Right to opt out of the sale of personal data
  • Right to opt out of profiling based on automated decision-making
  • Right to access personal data
  • Right to correct inaccuracies
  • Right to delete personal data
  • Right to obtain a copy of personal data

12.5 Connecticut Data Privacy Act (CTDPA)

For Connecticut residents, we provide similar rights as other US state privacy laws.

12.6 Brazil Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (LGPD)

For Brazilian users, we comply with LGPD:

  • Rights to confirmation of processing existence
  • Right to access and correct data
  • Right to anonymization, blocking, or deletion of unnecessary data
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to deletion of processed data with consent
  • Right to information about sharing with third parties
  • Right to consent withdrawal

12.7 Australia Privacy Act

For Australian users, we comply with the Privacy Act 1988:

  • Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) compliance
  • Open and transparent data collection practices
  • Rights to access and correct personal information
  • Complaint handling procedures

12.8 Canada Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA)

For Canadian users, we comply with PIPEDA:

  • Consent for collection, use, and disclosure
  • Limited collection of personal information
  • Individual access to personal information
  • Ability to challenge accuracy and completeness

12.9 Japan Act on Protection of Personal Information (APPI)

For Japanese users, we comply with APPI:

  • Clear disclosure of purpose of use
  • Restrictions on provision to third parties
  • Appropriate security measures

12.10 South Korea Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA)
